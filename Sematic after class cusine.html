Korean Cuisine

Korean food is known for its diverse flavors, healthy components, and communal dining style. Dishes often feature rice, vegetables, seafood, and meat, with a wide array of side dishes (banchan) accompanying the main course. Fermentation is a key aspect of Korean cuisine, with dishes like kimchi and gochujang (fermented red chili paste) being staples. The cuisine also emphasizes harmony and balance, with meals often featuring contrasting flavors and temperatures.

In any neighbourhood in Seoul you can find a spot specialising in cold noodles, or naengmyeon – literally a combination of naeng (cold) and myeon (noodles).
